The Web is a Place of Equality

Our website is the most open and democratic way for the U.S. citizenry to reach our content.
The web is accessible by default.
Accessibility is designed into the HTML spec.
It’s up to us to make sure we don’t mess it up.

The Web is a Place of Freedom

Our users have the most freedom
of getting to our content.

All you need is a browser. No specific device needed.

PBS is free to act in the way that is best for its users and our member stations

There are no gatekeepers or approval processes that we don't control.

The web is our proving ground and where most features are first to market.

  • CDM - Core Data Model
  • Franchises
  • GA Livestream
  • Continuous Play
  • Topics

As a developer,
I have the most freedom
in how I get to build things

No one company controls the raw materials of the web - HTML, CSS, JavaScript. As opposed to Java (Oracle/Android) and Swift (Apple).
Some implementations may be more controlled,
but they are changeable

->

Example: moving from JW Player to Video.js

The Web is a Place of Resilience

No other platform allows for such
progressive enhancement

allowing all users a core experience, while more and more capable devices get better and better experiences

The Power of a URL

The power of a single link to a single location
cannot be overstated.

<a href="https://www.pbs.org/">PBS</a>
Links are what make the web the web. Links are the lingua franca of the web. They're findable, searchable, shareable. They can get you to where you want to go in the fastest way possible. No download steps required. Deep links sooooorta work like this too, but those must fit within confined structures. Just ask an SEO expert how important links are.
